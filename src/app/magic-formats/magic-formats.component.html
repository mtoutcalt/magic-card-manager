Formats of Magic: The Gathering




Standard
The most popular format for Magic. It involves a player making a pre-constructed deck out of only the most recent 2 years worth of cards.

  • Deck must be at least 60 cards with no maximum size
  • You can use 15 cards for a sideboard - which are not in play but can be switched with your deck
  • Include up to 4 of each card
  • Reference

Modern
Similar to Standard but includes more sets that are acceptable. While Standard typically includes the last 2 years of sets Modern accepts Standard sets plus many years before - anything after Legacy.

  • Deck must be at least 60 cards with no maximum size
  • You can use 15 cards for a sideboard - which are not in play but can be switched with your deck
  • Include up to 4 of each card
  • Reference

Commander
This is a much different format which includes a chosen Hero and building a deck around them. The commander is a legendary creature and this format can have more than 2 players and the last player alive wins.

  • Deck consists of a legendary creature and 99 other cards
  • Can only have 1 copy of each card
  • All cards must follow the color of the commander
  • There can be 2-6 players in free-for-all and everyone starts with 40 life
  • Reference

Booster Draft
This format is popular at Tournaments. While other formats involve a pre-constructed deck, this format involves players opening new, random packs and building a new deck on the spot. Players sit around each other, open a new pack of cards, choose a single card and then pass the rest to their left. Packs are continually opened and passed around until each player has a full deck.

  • Deck must be at least 40 cards
  • Deck can have as many lands as needed
  • Reference

Legacy
This format is the only one that allows the use of Magic cards from all generations and all sets. It is similar to Standard and Modern but has opens up the use of the older sets before the Modern era. With that said, consult the offical Magic website to see the cards that are banned from play.

  • Deck must be at least 60 cards with no maximum size
  • You can use 15 cards for a sideboard - which are not in play but can be switched with your deck
  • Include up to 4 of each card
  • Reference
